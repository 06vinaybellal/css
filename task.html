Chatrapathi Shivaji Maharaj ji

His reign promoted religious toleration and the functional integration of various communities, leaving behind a legacy of courage, justice, and a spirit of independence that continues to inspire people.

Subhash chandra bose

Bose reportedly died in a plane crash in Taiwan on August 18, 1945, though the circumstances remain debated and surrounded by mystery. Known as Netaji, Bose’s legacy is celebrated across India, and he is revered for his courage, vision, and sacrifice for the nation.

Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh was a revolutionary Indian freedom fighter known for his militant opposition to British colonial rule. Born in a Sikh family in Punjab, he became a key figure in the Indian independence movement through radical actions and inspiring youth activism.

Alluri Setharama Raju

Alluri Sitarama Raju was a legendary Indian revolutionary and freedom fighter who led the Rampa Rebellion against British rule in the Eastern Ghats region of Andhra Pradesh between 1922 and 1924

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was a key leader in the Indian independence movement and the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of independent India, renowned for his role in unifying the country and integrating princely states into the Indian Union.

Bal Gangadhar Tilak

Tilak blended religious revivalism with political activism, using Hindu cultural symbols to inspire nationalist fervor while emphasizing Indian pride and resistance to westernization.

Sarojini Naidu

Sarojini Naidu was a distinguished Indian poet, freedom fighter, and political leader often called the "Nightingale of India" for her lyrical poetry and eloquence. She played a significant role in India’s struggle for independence and became the first Indian woman to become the president of the Indian National Congress
